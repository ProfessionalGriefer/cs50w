The Piano

My favourite pieces

I have played all of the following pieces. A few comments on each masterpiece.

Chopin

My absolute favourite composer as you can see by the length of this section.

Revolutionary Etude (Op. 10 No. 12)

The only Chopin etude I've played. Memorizing the left hand is very tedious. Many sections are very unusual and feel very different from other pieces I've played. But if you've played the piece for a while and memorized it completely then it is very satisfying to play.

Nocturne in C Sharp Minor (No. 20)

Around 2 weeks before my first piano competition(25. - 26. January 2014 Jugend Musiziert) my piano teacher told me to play this piece. It was then really hard because I've just played the piano for 2 years but it was the most beautiful I've ever played. Because I couldn't play this piece well enough my teacher blew it off.

Nocturne in E Flat Major (Op. 9 No. 2)

One of Chopin's most iconic piece. I played this piece around 2 years ago and it is very easy but still sounds nice. It gets more boring if you play it too often.

Fantaisie-Impromptu (Op. 66)

The first piece for me that uses left and right hand at a completely different rhythm. I wanted to play this piece after seeing it on YouTube. Honestly, it was pretty easy. First, play the right hand only. Second, play the left hand only. Third, merge them together. It will sound pretty messy but the rhythm is the only difficult part of the piano piece.

Ballade No. 1 in G Minor

I'm still learning this piece and it takes eternity until I am able to play it perfectly. 12 Sites long with complete different melodies and sections on each page and has the complete range of emotions. The beginning may sound easy but it definitely isn't.

I could embed a video for every piano piece but I'm too lazy to google all of them.

Liszt

Another romantic-era pianist. I wanted to play Hungarian Rhapsody No. 2 and La Campanella and quickly moved on after seeing the notes. Liszt's music is also very beautiful but it is a lot harder than Chopin's. The fingering is absolutely weird and not fun to practice.

Liebestraum Nr. 3

I love this piece (no pun intended). First encounter with "real" cadenzas.

Un Sospiro

The sheet music has three bars on it at the beginning and because of it the notes are tiny. The beginning is very easy but the middle section was extremely difficult. My piano teacher just sat there and said: "Good, good" with no further comments. That is maybe because I'm the only student playing an obsessive amount of romantic-era music.

Beethoven

Thousand times better than Amadeus. Mozart's melodies are too repetitive and everyone will notice even if you miss a single note. The same goes for Haydn. I had a bad experience with Beethoven in my second piano competition. I've messed up a Sonatine so extremely that I had to start again. And then I've messed up again and again and again. One of the professors walked up and told me to relax, try again and if it doesn't work then move on. My mother had to walk up and bring me the music book. After that I had this shaking which ruined my last piece. I still got the 2nd place with 20 points (21 - 25 points = 1st place). The Schubert improvisation saved me. I still wanted the first place so badly to prove another music professor wrong who told me that I was only able to achieve 3rd place with his help.

Für Elise

Who saw that coming? I think that is the most iconic piece in the piano repertoire. If I sit in front of the piano and prepare to play I hear: "Play Für Elise" oftentimes. It was the first piece of my first piano competition. I tried to play it too quietly and messed a few notes up.

Mondschein Sonate 3rd Movement

It is very fast pace, vivid and I love it. The complete opposite to the 1st movement.
