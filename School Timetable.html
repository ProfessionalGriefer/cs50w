My Timetable

My current timetable for the EF. EF is German and is the abbreviation for Einf√ºhrungsphase or introductory phase. This school year's function is to prepare you for the Q1 and Q2, two school years where you collect points for your Abitur. After your final exams your collected points represent a grade.

The maximum amount of points is 900 which represents a 1,0. In Germany you write a comma instead of a dot to display float values and a dot instead of a comma to seperate groups of 3 digits. In the American school system a Grade Point Average (GPA) of 4.0 represents a perfect score but in Germany it means the you have barely made it.

The lessons are 45 minutes each and 90 minutes for a double lesson. Each lesson is seperated by a 5 minutes break and each double lesson is seperated by a 20 minutes break. After the 6th lesson we get a 60 minutes break. In my opinion this is too much. I rather go earlier home to advance in the CS50X lessons.

In the grades 5 to 10, the so called Unterstufe (lower grade) you get a substitution teacher if your teacher is missing. But in the Oberstufe (upper grades?) it is a free hour. You can stay in school in the Oberstufenraum (upper grades room?) or you can go home. At some days you have so many free hours that you only come for the first two hours for example.

Subjects assessment

I really love all the Naturwissenschaften (sciences). Math is great, but way too easy (boring). Chemistry and Biology are great. My grades are A for both but I have to kick one out to avoid having too many hours per week. I chose Chemistry over Biology because I don't want to memorize thousands of different technical terms. If you understand the topic in Chemistry then you don't have to learn at all. The Social Sciences (SW) is together with English my advanced course. I really love economy and politics (Sociology is okay). Writing English text is not very hard but writing it in a eloquent and gramatically correct way is a challenge on it's own.
